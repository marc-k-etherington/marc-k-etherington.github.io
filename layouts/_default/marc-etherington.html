Academic Journey

Originally from Stockton-on-Tees, I completed my MPhys at Durham University (2007-2011) before pursuing a PhD in Physics at Clare College, Cambridge under Prof. Neil Greenham (2011-2015). My doctoral research focused on spin polarization in organic photovoltaics.

I returned to Durham as a Research Associate (2015-2019), working with Prof. Andy Monkman on EU Horizon 2020 projects (PHEBE and HyperOLED). During this period, I made key discoveries about the spin-vibronic coupling mechanism in TADF and persistent dimer emission - work that has fundamentally shaped the field.

In 2020, I joined Northumbria University as a Vice-Chancellor's Senior Fellow, becoming Assistant Professor in 2022. I now lead the Etherington Lab and collaborate closely with Dr Jon Knowles in the Knowles-Etherington Group, combining synthetic and spectroscopic expertise to tackle fundamental challenges in organic light-emitting materials.
